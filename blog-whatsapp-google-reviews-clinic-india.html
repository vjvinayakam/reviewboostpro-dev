How to Get Your Google Review Link

You need a direct link that takes patients straight to the review form — not just your Google Business listing. Here's how to find it:

  1. Go to business.google.com and sign in to your account
  2. Click on your clinic profile
  3. Click "Ask for reviews" or "Get more reviews"
  4. Copy the short link provided
  5. Use this link in all your WhatsApp messages

This link skips the search step entirely and opens the review form directly on the patient's phone. That small reduction in friction makes a huge difference in how many people actually complete the review.

Sending to Multiple Patients — The Weekly Batch Method

Once you're comfortable sending individually, scale up by uploading a CSV file with patient names and phone numbers. Many clinic owners do this every Friday evening as a weekly batch — sending review requests to all patients seen that week.

  • Create a spreadsheet with two columns: name and phone
  • Export as CSV
  • Upload the CSV to your review tool
  • The system opens WhatsApp for each patient with the message pre-filled
  • You hit Send for each one — sent from your own number, one by one

Takes about 10 minutes for 20–30 patients. Done once a week, this alone generates 3–8 new reviews every week consistently.

What to Do When You Get a Negative Review

Despite the smart routing system, occasionally a patient will leave a low rating directly on Google. Here's how to handle it professionally:

  1. Respond within 24 hours — speed signals that you care
  2. Acknowledge the concern without arguing or being defensive
  3. Offer to resolve it offline — share your phone or email
  4. Never share patient details publicly in your response
  5. Flag fake reviews to Google for removal (spam, off-topic, competitor)
"Thank you for sharing your feedback. We're sorry your experience didn't meet your expectations. We take all patient feedback seriously and would like to understand what happened. Please contact us at [phone/email] so we can make this right." — Example response that builds trust with future patients

A professional response to a negative review often builds more trust than the bad review damages. Future patients see how you handled it.

Is Asking Patients for Reviews Allowed?

Yes — asking patients for honest reviews is completely allowed under Google's policies. What is NOT allowed:

  • Paying patients to leave reviews
  • Offering discounts or gifts in exchange for reviews
  • Posting fake reviews from staff or friends accounts
  • Asking only happy patients (review gating) — which is why our system sends all ratings through the same link

Sending a polite WhatsApp message to a real patient after a real visit is 100% within Google's guidelines. Thousands of Indian clinics do this every day.
